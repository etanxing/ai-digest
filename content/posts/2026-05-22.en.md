## 1. OpenAI ships GPT-5 with reasoning built in by default

OpenAI quietly flipped reasoning on by default across the GPT-5 family this week, removing the separate "o-series" tier users had to opt into. The company says the change reduces latency by 40% on common coding and writing tasks compared to GPT-4o with explicit reasoning enabled.

The move intensifies pressure on competitors who still charge separately for extended thinking time. Anthropic and Google have not yet announced a comparable consolidation.

Sources: [OpenAI blog](https://openai.com), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com)

## 2. Google DeepMind releases Gemini 2.5 Ultra with 2M-token context

DeepMind's latest Gemini update extends the context window to two million tokens — enough to ingest an entire codebase or a full run of a long-form document series in a single prompt. Early benchmarks on long-document QA put it ahead of all publicly available competitors.

The technical report credits a new sparse attention mechanism that keeps inference costs flat even at full context length, addressing the quadratic scaling problem that has plagued long-context models.

Sources: [Google AI blog](https://ai.google.dev), [MIT Technology Review](https://technologyreview.com), [HuggingFace papers](https://huggingface.co)

## 3. Meta open-sources Llama 4 Scout, a 17B mixture-of-experts model

Meta released Llama 4 Scout under its custom open-license today, a 17B activated-parameter model drawn from a 109B total parameter mixture-of-experts architecture. On reasoning benchmarks it trades blows with GPT-4 mini at a fraction of the compute cost.

The release reignites the open vs. closed model debate, with Mistral and Cohere quickly announcing fine-tuned variants. Cloudflare Workers AI added Scout to its inference catalog within hours of release.

Sources: [Meta AI](https://ai.meta.com), [The Verge](https://theverge.com), [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co)
