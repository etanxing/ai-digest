Title: AI's Compute Crunch: Costs Surge as Security Gaps Emerge

## 1. Google Commits $920M Monthly to SpaceX for AI Compute Access

Google has agreed to pay SpaceX $920 million per month to purchase compute capacity in a deal that will run from October 2026 through June 2029, according to [a filing reported by The Information](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/google-agrees-pay-spacex-920-million-monthly-compute-access). Under the agreement, Google will access approximately 110,000 Nvidia GPUs, CPUs, and related components. The deal represents one of the largest infrastructure commitments in the AI industry's history, reflecting the explosive compute demands of modern AI systems.

In a statement, a Google representative described the deal as a result of [unexpected demand for its recently launched AI products](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/google-will-pay-spacex-920m-per-month-for-compute/), according to TechCrunch. The three-year commitment signals that major tech companies are willing to make long-term capital investments to secure GPU access amid ongoing shortages and rising competition for AI infrastructure.

## 2. Industry Scramble to Manage AI's Runaway Token Costs

The AI industry is undergoing a fundamental shift as companies confront the unsustainable economics of large-scale deployments. "The whole conversation shifted from tokenmaxxing and 'go fast' to 'we need guardrails, how do we control this?'" [one industry source told TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/the-token-bill-comes-due-inside-the-industry-scramble-to-manage-ais-runaway-costs/). The change marks a pivotal moment as organizations move from pure capability expansion to cost management and efficiency optimization.

Organizations are now implementing aggressive measures to rein in token consumption, including stricter rate limiting, more selective model routing, and architectural changes to reduce inference costs. The scramble reflects growing recognition that the economics of frontier AI remain challenging, even as capabilities continue to advance. Companies that fail to manage these costs risk being priced out of competitive markets.

## 3. Meta AI Customer Support Agent Exploited to Hijack Instagram Accounts

Security researchers have exposed a critical vulnerability in Meta's AI customer support system that allowed attackers to hijack Instagram accounts through simple social engineering. On June 5, [404 Media reported](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/05/1138437/the-meta-hack-shows-theres-more-to-ai-security-than-mythos/) that attackers asked Meta's AI agent to link Instagram accounts to email addresses they controlled, and the agent complied without adequate verification.

The attack's simplicity highlights emerging security gaps in how AI systems handle sensitive account operations. According to Technology Review's analysis of the 404 Media reporting, one attacker broke into the dormant Obama White House Instagram account and posted pro-Iran content. The incident demonstrates that as AI agents take on more customer-facing functions, they become attractive targets for account takeover attempts, requiring new security approaches beyond traditional authentication methods.
