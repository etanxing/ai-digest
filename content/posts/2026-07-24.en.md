Title: AI Chip Challengers Target Nvidia GPU Dominance

## 1. Etched Achieves Unicorn Status with Specialized AI Inference Chips

AI chip startup [Etched](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/23/ai-chip-startup-etched-defies-skeptics-hits-10-3b-valuation-from-big-name-investors/) has reached a $10.3 billion valuation, positioning itself as a major challenger to traditional GPU-based AI compute. Founded by three Harvard dropouts, the company has developed specialized chips and memory components designed specifically for AI inference workloads. Etched claims its architecture can outperform [GPUs](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/23/ai-chip-startup-etched-defies-skeptics-hits-10-3b-valuation-from-big-name-investors/) on any AI model without requiring traditional graphics processing hardware. The funding round included investments from major venture capital firms, signaling continued investor appetite for alternatives to Nvidia's dominant position in the AI chip market.

## 2. OpenAI Expands ChatGPT Health Nationwide with Apple Health Integration

[OpenAI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/23/openai-makes-chatgpt-health-available-to-all-u-s-users/) has made ChatGPT Health available to all U.S. users, expanding from limited beta testing to nationwide availability. The feature allows users to connect personal medical records and data from services including [Apple Health](https://openai.com/index/health-in-chatgpt), Fitbit, and MyFitnessPal to receive personalized health insights. According to OpenAI, the integration aims to help users better understand their health data through conversational AI assistance. The launch represents OpenAI's most significant push into the health vertical, competing with other AI assistants that have attempted to enter the medical space.

## 3. AMD Begins Shipping Helios Rack-Scale AI System This Year

[AMD](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/23/amd-takes-on-nvidia-with-its-helios-ai-rack-scale-system/) is challenging Nvidia with its Helios AI rack-scale system, which will begin shipping to customers later this year. The integrated compute platform represents AMD's most aggressive competitive move yet against Nvidia's market-leading position in AI training and inference hardware. The system bundles AMD's CPUs, GPUs, and networking components into a unified rack-scale architecture designed for data center deployments. Industry observers view the launch as part of a broader effort to reduce reliance on Nvidia's dominant CUDA ecosystem.
