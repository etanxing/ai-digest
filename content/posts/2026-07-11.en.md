Title: AI Giants Clash as $26.5B IPO Signals Memory Boom

## 1. Apple Sues OpenAI Over Alleged Trade Secret Theft

Apple has filed a lawsuit against OpenAI accusing the AI company of orchestrating a systematic campaign to steal confidential hardware specifications and supplier information through employee recruitment, according to [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/). The lawsuit alleges the misconduct was [directed by OpenAI's senior leadership](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-trade-secret-theft/), including a long-time former Apple employee who joined OpenAI.

According to [Wired](https://www.wired.com/story/apple-sues-openai-allegedly-stealing-ip-hardware/), Apple claims OpenAI encouraged poached employees to bring over confidential presentations, secret prototypes, and key supplier details. The iPhone maker is seeking injunctive relief and damages, signaling an escalation in intellectual property disputes between major technology companies racing to develop advanced AI systems.

## 2. OpenAI Launches GPT 5.6 Family; New Models Power Microsoft Copilot

OpenAI unveiled its GPT 5.6 model family on Thursday, featuring Sol, Terra, and Luna variants designed for different enterprise use cases, according to [Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-openai-launches-gpt-56-solterraluna). The new models will continue to power Microsoft's suite of workplace and productivity apps, with OpenAI describing GPT 5.6 as the ["preferred model" for Microsoft Copilot 365](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/09/openai-says-gpt-5-6-is-the-preferred-model-for-microsoft-copilot-amid-breakup-chatter/) amid ongoing reports of a potential breakup between the two companies.

The release comes as OpenAI navigates IPO speculation and intensifying enterprise competition. The company also released a [mathematical proof demonstrating GPT-5.6's advanced reasoning capabilities](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/04d1d1e4-bc75-476a-97cf-49055cd98d31/cdc_proof.pdf), solving the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture, a longstanding problem in graph theory.

## 3. SK Hynix Raises $26.5B in Largest Foreign IPO in US History

SK Hynix has raised $26.5 billion in the biggest foreign IPO in US history, marking the AI chip boom's biggest Wall Street moment yet, according to [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/sk-hynix-raises-26-5b-in-the-biggest-foreign-ipo-in-us-history-is-urged-to-build-new-us-fabs/). The South Korean memory chipmaker, which supplies high-bandwidth memory to AI data centers, saw strong institutional demand amid surging interest in AI infrastructure.

Lawmakers and industry observers are urging both SK Hynix and Samsung to build new US fabrication facilities as the semiconductor industry grapples with geopolitical supply chain concerns and the CHIPS Act's push for domestic production.
