Title: AI Giants Launch Competing Models, Expand Enterprise Reach

## 1. OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6 Family, Deepens Microsoft Partnership

OpenAI has released its [GPT-5.6 model family](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-6), comprising three tiers—Luna, Terra, and Sol—now serving as the [preferred model for Microsoft 365 Copilot](https://openai.com/index/gpt-5-6-preferred-model-microsoft-365-copilot). The rollout marks a significant escalation in the OpenAI-Microsoft partnership, integrating stronger AI capabilities across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and collaborative tools. The new models promise improvements across reasoning, cybersecurity applications, and general performance, according to [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/09/openai-launches-its-new-family-of-models-with-gpt-5-6/).

Pricing positions the family competitively: Luna at $1/$6 per million input/output tokens, Terra at $2.50/$15, and Sol at $5/$30. This directly undercuts Anthropic's Claude Opus series at $5/$25 and the upcoming Claude Fable 5 at $10/$50. However, [observers note](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/9/gpt-5-6/#atom-everything) that raw token pricing has become less meaningful as reasoning token counts vary widely between models for equivalent tasks.

## 2. Anthropic Unveils 'Jacobian Lens' for AI Interpretability

Anthropic has developed a technique offering [the clearest view yet into large language model internals](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/07/09/1140293/anthropic-found-a-hidden-space-where-claude-puzzles-over-concepts/). The "Jacobian lens" tool allows researchers to observe how models process concepts and execute tasks, revealing unexpected behaviors that Anthropic describes as ranging from mundane observations to "unnerving" findings about AI cognition. The research represents a step forward in mechanistic interpretability, an area critical for understanding and ensuring AI safety.

The technique exposes hidden representations within Claude, showing how the model manipulates concepts during reasoning. While Anthropic has not released full technical details, the company says the method provides unprecedented transparency into decision-making processes. This interpretability work comes as Anthropic faces intensifying competition from OpenAI's latest releases and prepares for a potential IPO.

## 3. Meta Enters AI Coding Market with Muse Spark 1.1

Meta has launched [Muse Spark 1.1](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/09/meta-enters-the-crowded-ai-coding-battle-with-muse-spark-1-1/), its first Spark model to offer a public API, positioning itself against established AI coding assistants. The model targets enterprise automation with improvements in agentic tool calling and computer use capabilities, handling large workloads, bug fixes, and code migrations. According to Meta, the release addresses growing demand for AI-driven development automation.

The launch follows [Muse Spark in April](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/9/muse-spark-1-1/#atom-everything) and introduces a capability the company calls "Attractor States in Self-Conversation"—where two model instances interact, producing unexpected outputs like "My whole existence is a waiting room by design." The API availability marks Meta's push into the developer market, competing directly with tools from OpenAI, Anthropic, and specialized coding assistants.
