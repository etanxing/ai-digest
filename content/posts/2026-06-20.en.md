Title: US Blocks Anthropic Models as India Deploys AI at Scale

## 1. US Forces Anthropic to Pull Fable 5, Mythos 5 Over Guardrail Concerns

The US government [forced Anthropic to pull](https://techcrunch.com/video/is-the-us-governments-anthropic-ban-accidentally-helping-the-brand/) its newest models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5, citing national security concerns after [Amazon researchers allegedly found a way to bypass Fable 5's guardrails](https://techcrunch.com/podcast/the-us-banned-anthropics-fable-5-release-but-the-numbers-dont-seem-to-care/). The intervention marks an unprecedented escalation in government oversight of AI model releases. Cybersecurity researchers have since [signed an open letter](https://techcrunch.com/video/is-the-us-governments-anthropic-ban-accidentally-helping-the-brand/) calling the move dangerous, arguing it could hinder legitimate security research. Anthropic itself noted that the same jailbreaks exist in other models on the market, raising questions about why these specific models were targeted. The incident has reignited debate over how governments should balance national security concerns with open research and innovation in AI development.

## 2. Miami Startup Subquadratic Claims Breakthrough on Decade-Old LLM Bottleneck

[Miami-based AI startup Subquadratic](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/06/19/1139313/a-startup-claims-it-broke-through-a-bottleneck-thats-holding-back-llms/) emerged from stealth mode last month with a significant claim: solving a mathematical bottleneck holding back large language models for almost a decade. The company has started sharing evidence to support its assertion, though details remain limited. If verified, the breakthrough would address the "flash attention" problem—a core computational constraint in transformer-based AI training. Such a solution could unlock meaningful efficiency gains for training and inference across the industry. Subquadratic is preparing to release technical documentation backing its claims, which could either solidify its credibility or face scrutiny from researchers skeptical of unproven assertions.

## 3. Reliance to Embed AI Across 500M+ User Telecom Network Under Ambani's Vision

[Reliance Industries](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/19/billionaire-ambani-wants-ai-in-every-call-app-and-home/) is weaving AI into telecom services used by more than 500 million people, representing one of the most ambitious commercial AI deployments attempted by any single company globally. Under Mukesh Ambani's vision, the initiative aims to embed artificial intelligence into every call, app, and home connected through Reliance's network. The company's Jio platforms serve the vast majority of India's mobile users, giving the deployment an unparalleled reach into a massive population. Industry observers view the project as a test case for whether AI can be meaningfully integrated into consumer telecommunications at scale without degrading service quality or raising privacy concerns.
