Title: Tech giants bet billions to own AI infrastructure future

## 1. Microsoft launches its own AI deployment company with $2.5 billion commitment

Microsoft announced a $2.5 billion commitment to launch its own AI deployment company, following similar moves by Amazon, OpenAI, and Anthropic into dedicated AI infrastructure services. The investment signals that the enterprise AI deployment market has reached critical mass, as major tech companies race to capture the infrastructure layer of the AI boom. Microsoft's new division will focus on helping businesses deploy and manage AI models at scale, competing directly with cloud rivals who have already launched comparable services.

The move underscores how AI infrastructure has become the new battleground for Big Tech. Companies that control the underlying compute and deployment layers stand to capture significant value as AI adoption accelerates across industries. Microsoft's deep enterprise relationships through Azure give it a potential advantage in selling AI deployment services to its existing customer base. The investment is among the largest single commitments to AI infrastructure by a major cloud provider this year.

## 2. OpenAI proposed donating 5% of its equity to a US sovereign wealth fund

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has reportedly proposed giving 5% of the company's equity to a U.S. sovereign wealth fund, reviving discussions about letting the public share in the financial gains from the AI boom. The proposal could establish a new model for how frontier AI companies balance commercial interests with national benefit. The plan would represent a significant structural shift in how AI profits flow back to the public, rather than remaining concentrated among investors and employees.

The proposal comes as AI companies face increasing scrutiny over their economic impact and concentration of power. A sovereign wealth fund stake would give American taxpayers a direct financial interest in AI prosperity. Altman has previously discussed various structures to ensure AI benefits are more widely distributed, but this represents his most concrete proposal to date. The idea echoes proposals from other tech leaders who have suggested that gains from transformative technologies should be more broadly shared.

## 3. Anthropic is discussing a new custom chip with Samsung

Anthropic is in discussions with Samsung about developing a new custom AI chip, according to sources familiar with the matter. The news comes about a week after OpenAI announced its own custom AI chip in a partnership with Broadcom. The industry-wide race to control AI hardware has intensified as companies seek to reduce dependence on Nvidia and differentiate their next-generation models. Custom chips could provide performance improvements and cost savings for training and inference at scale.

The talks with Samsung, a major semiconductor manufacturer, would give Anthropic access to advanced chip manufacturing capabilities. Anthropic has previously worked with various chip partners but has not disclosed a dedicated custom silicon arrangement. The company recently raised significant funding and has signaled that infrastructure investment is a priority. This move highlights how AI safety-focused companies are also pursuing aggressive hardware strategies to remain competitive.
