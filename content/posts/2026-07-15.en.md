Title: AI Hardware Debut, Siri Opens, Regulation Calls

## 1. OpenAI Reportedly Building a Moving, Screenless Companion Speaker

OpenAI is developing its first physical product: a mobile, screenless companion speaker with [mechanical elements that can move on their own](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/openais-first-hardware-device-is-reportedly-a-screenless-speaker-that-can-move/), according to a Bloomberg report. The device is designed to "feel like a companion and become a physical manifestation of OpenAI's ChatGPT," marking a significant strategic diversification beyond the company's software roots. The move positions OpenAI alongside rivals like Amazon and Google, who have already established presence in the smart home speaker market.

The reported device represents a notable pivot for a company that has built its reputation entirely on software and API services. Industry observers suggest the hardware push could help OpenAI establish a more direct consumer relationship, moving beyond its reliance on partnerships and API distribution. No timeline or pricing details have been disclosed, and OpenAI has not officially confirmed the project.

## 2. Apple Releases iOS 27 Public Beta, Opening Revamped Siri to All iPhone Users

Apple has released the [iOS 27 public beta](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/apple-opens-its-new-siri-ai-to-everyone-with-the-ios-27-public-beta/), giving all iPhone owners early access to its AI-powered Siri assistant ahead of the official fall launch. Users who previously had to install a developer beta to test the revamped assistant can now access it through Apple's public testing program. The release democratizes access to on-device AI features that Apple has been developing as part of its broader Apple Intelligence initiative.

The public beta includes the redesigned Siri interface and new AI capabilities that Apple showcased at its Worldwide Developers Conference. The move signals Apple's commitment to making AI features accessible to its massive user base before the polished fall release. iOS 27 is expected to ship widely to consumers in September alongside new iPhone hardware.

## 3. DeepMind CEO Proposes Independent AI Standards Body for Frontier Model Regulation

DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis is proposing an [AI standards body modeled after FINRA](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/deepmind-ceo-calls-for-an-independent-standards-body-to-regulate-frontier-ai/) to test frontier models and develop best practices for their release. The regulatory framework would operate independently from both government agencies and individual companies, aiming to establish consistent safety and capability benchmarks across the industry. Hassabis's proposal reflects growing consensus among major AI labs that external oversight is needed as capabilities advance.

The proposed body would conduct standardized testing of frontier AI models before release, similar to how financial regulators assess broker-dealer practices. Hassabis joins other AI leaders who have publicly supported some form of third-party evaluation, though specifics around funding, membership, and enforcement authority remain unsettled. The initiative comes as policymakers in the US, EU, and Asia continue debating competing approaches to AI governance.
